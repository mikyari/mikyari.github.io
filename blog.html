What Exactly is Waste Segregation

    Waste Segregation

  1. Waste segregation means dividing and selecting of waste materials into dry and wet
  2. Wet waste: this are organic waste usually generated by eating establishment and they are heavy in weight due to dampness
  3. Dry waste: this includes wood and related materials, metals and glass.

Steps to waste segregation Keep separate containers for dry and wet waste in the kitchen. Keep two bags for dry waste collection and paper and plastic, for the rest of the household waste. Keep plastic from the kitchen clean and drop into the dry waste bin. Send wet waste out of your home daily, store and send dry waste out of the home, once a week. Keep a paper bag for disposing sanitary waste. .
